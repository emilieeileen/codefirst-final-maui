Geography




While all the Hawaiians Islands have unique geography, Maui is unique. Comprised of one extinct volcano and one dormant, this island has a lush and vaired landscape. The Western side of the island has pristine and luxurious beaches, such as the famous KƒÅ'anapali Beach. The East side is even more lush, with tropical rainforests surrounding the small town of Hana. The South side of the island is mainly comprised of the Southern slopes of Haeleakala. The volcano creates a rain shield, creating a volcanic landscape similar to Iceland.

Maui also has unique geography off its shores. Many know of the coral reefs surrounding the island but did you know there's a another volcanic feature just off shore? Molokini is a partially submerged lava vent that sits just south of Maui. Today, it is a great spot to snorkel and look out for tropical fish and turtles!
