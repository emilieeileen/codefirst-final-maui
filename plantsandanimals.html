Plants and Animals




The Hawaiian Islands are home to thousands of unique plants and animals. A few, however, seem to frequently come to mind when thinking of Hawaii. The state flower is the Hibiscus, which can be found all across the Hawaiian Islands. The state bird is the Nēnē. Most commonly found on the Island of Kauai, you can only find this bird on the slops of the Haeleakala Volcano. The state tree is the Kukui tree. This tree produces kukui nuts which are used in the kukui nut leis traditionally worn by men.

Unsurprisingly, most of the unique animals found in Maui are in the sea. If you snorkel in one of the many coral reefs around the island you will find loads of tropical fish and turtles. Known in the Hawaiian language as honu, turtles also are an important symbol in Hawaiian culture. If you visit the islands during the winter months, scan the waters for humpback whales. You will surely spot a breaching whale as you enjoy your catamoran cruise!
